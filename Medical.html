[Self Checkup]


1.Body Tempreature:

Using a thermometer to check for fever, which can indicate infections or other illnesses.

2. Blood Pressure:

Using a blood pressure monitor to check blood pressure levels. High blood pressure can be a sign of heart problems or hypertension.

3. Heart Rate:

Monitoring pulse rate to ensure it falls within a healthy range. Irregularities might indicate heart issues.

4.Breast Self-Examination:

Checking breasts for lumps, changes in size or shape, or any other abnormalities that could indicate breast cancer.

5. Skin Examination:

Regularly checking moles, freckles, or any other skin changes that might suggest skin cancer.

6. Oral Health Check:

Regularly examining the mouth and gums for signs of cavities, gum disease, or oral cancer.

7. Mental Health Check:

Monitoring one's own mood, stress levels, or changes in behavior that might indicate mental health issues like depression or anxiety.

8. Diet and Exercise:

Monitoring one's own diet and exercise routines to ensure a healthy lifestyle.

9. Monitoring Chronic Conditions:

For individuals with chronic conditions (such as diabetes), self-checkups might involve monitoring blood sugar levels or other specific parameters.

[Health Tips & Guidance]

Explore valuable health tips and Guidance.

1.Balanced diet

Eat a variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Limit processed foods, sugary drinks, and excessive amounts of caffeine. Control portion sizes to maintain a healthy weight.

2. Regular Exercise:

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous activity per week. Include strength training exercises at least twice a week.

3: Adequate Sleep::

Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep per night to allow your body to rest and rejuvenate..

4. Stress Management:

Practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing, meditation, or yoga. Engage in hobbies and activities that bring you joy and relaxation.

6. Avoid Harmful Substances:

Limit alcohol consumption and avoid smoking or recreational drug use.

7. Regular Health Check-ups:

Schedule regular check-ups with your healthcare provider for screenings and vaccinations. Monitor your blood pressure, cholesterol levels, and other health markers regularly.

8. Mental Health:

Prioritize your mental health. Seek help if you are feeling persistently sad, anxious, or overwhelmed. Talk to friends, family, or a mental health professional if you need support.

9. Practice Good Hygiene:

Wash your hands frequently to prevent the spread of germs. Maintain good oral hygiene by brushing and flossing your teeth regularly.

10. Safety Measures:

Use seat belts and helmets to prevent accidents and injuries. Follow safety guidelines at home and work to avoid accidents.

11. Limit Screen Time:

Reduce the time spent on electronic devices, especially before bedtime.
Take breaks during prolonged screen use to prevent eye strain. Stay Socially Connected:

12. Continuous Learning:

Stay Informed: Keep yourself updated about health issues, new research, and best practices for a healthy lifestyle.

Seek Professional Help: If you have specific health concerns or goals, consult healthcare professionals, nutritionists, or fitness trainers for personalized guidance.
